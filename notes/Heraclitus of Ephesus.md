## Summary
The philosopher of flux and fire. "You cannot step in the same river twice." Heraclitus proposed that the fundamental reality is not a substance but a process — constant change governed by an underlying rational principle he called the Logos. His work connects Egyptian Ma'at, Pre-Socratic natural philosophy, and the later Stoic tradition.
