# Anaximander of Miletus
`thinker` | c. 610–546 BCE | Greek (Milesian)
**Traditions:** Pre-Socratic Philosophy · Milesian School

## Summary
Student of Thales and the most philosophically sophisticated of the Milesians. Proposed the Apeiron — "the boundless" or "the indefinite" — as the arche, arguing that no particular thing (not even water) could be the fundamental principle, since everything has its opposite. The first known thinker to give a philosophical argument rather than just assert a position.
