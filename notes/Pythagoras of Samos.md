# Pythagoras of Samos
`thinker` | c. 570–495 BCE | Greek
**Traditions:** Pre-Socratic Philosophy · Mathematical Philosophy · Mysticism

## Summary
Proposed that the fundamental reality is number — or more precisely, ratio and mathematical proportion. Blended natural philosophy with religious mysticism (the Pythagorean brotherhood). His influence runs from Plato's mathematical idealism to modern mathematical physics. Reportedly studied in both Egypt and Babylon.
