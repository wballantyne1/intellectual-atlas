# Egyptian Wisdom Tradition
`tradition` | c. 3100 BCE – c. 400 CE | Northeast Africa (Nile Valley)

---

## Summary
One of the world's oldest and longest-running intellectual traditions. Its central concept — Ma'at — is one of the earliest articulations of a universal moral and cosmic law. Egyptian thought profoundly influenced early Greek philosophy; both Pythagoras and Plato are reported to have studied in Egypt. Alexandria later became the crucible in which Egyptian, Greek, and Jewish thought fused.

---

## Key Contributions
- [[Ma'at]]: cosmic order, truth, and justice as a universal principle
- Practical geometry and mathematics (Rhind Papyrus)
- Systematic empirical medicine (Edwin Smith Papyrus)
- Hermeticism: Egyptian-Greek philosophical fusion
- The Library of Alexandria
