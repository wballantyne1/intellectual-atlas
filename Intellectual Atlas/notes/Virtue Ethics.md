# Virtue Ethics
`idea` | First articulated: c. 400 BCE | Contested: yes
**Traditions:** Classical Greek Philosophy · Ethics · Moral Philosophy
**Primary thinker:** [[Aristotle]]

---

## Summary
The ethical framework that asks not "what should I do?" but "what kind of person should I be?" Virtues — courage, justice, temperance, wisdom — are stable character dispositions constituting human flourishing (eudaimonia). Displaced by Kantian and utilitarian ethics in the 18th century, revived as a major framework in the 20th.

---

## Connections
- Originated with: [[Socrates]] — virtue is knowledge; wrongdoing is ignorance
- Developed by: [[Plato]] — the Form of the Good as the foundation of virtue
- Fully systematised by: [[Aristotle]] — eudaimonia, the mean, habituation
- Challenged by: [[Immanuel Kant]] — duty and the categorical imperative over character
- Revived: 20th-century analytic philosophy — Anscombe, MacIntyre, Foot
