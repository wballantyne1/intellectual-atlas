# The Apeiron
`idea` | First articulated: c. 560 BCE | Contested: yes
**Traditions:** Pre-Socratic Philosophy · Metaphysics
**Primary thinker:** [[Anaximander of Miletus]]

---

## Summary
The boundless, indefinite first principle proposed by Anaximander. Not water, air, or any particular thing — but something prior to all opposites: unlimited, eternal, formless. The first fully abstract concept in Western philosophy, born from the first philosophical argument: any determinate substance has an opposite, so the arche must be prior to all opposites.
