# Zeno of Elea
`thinker` | c. 490–430 BCE | Greek (Eleatic)
**Traditions:** Pre-Socratic Philosophy · Eleatic School

> **Stub** — full node not yet built

## Summary
Student of Parmenides. Developed famous paradoxes — Achilles and the Tortoise, the Arrow — to defend the Parmenidean position that motion and multiplicity are illusions. The paradoxes were not resolved until the development of calculus and set theory in the 17th–19th centuries. The first known use of reductio ad absurdum as a philosophical method.
