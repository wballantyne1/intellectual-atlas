# Parmenides of Elea
`thinker` | c. 515–450 BCE | Greek (Eleatic)
**Traditions:** Pre-Socratic Philosophy · Eleatic School · Metaphysics

---

## Summary
The philosopher who argued that change is impossible and multiplicity is illusion. Only Being exists; Non-being cannot exist; therefore nothing comes into existence or passes away. This is the total contradiction of Heraclitus. The argument is pure logic — no appeal to evidence — and it is genuinely hard to escape. Every major philosopher after him has had to respond.

---

## Contradicts
- [[Heraclitus of Ephesus]] — total contradiction on the nature of change

## Influenced / Responded to by
- [[Plato]] — the Forms are Parmenidean (unchanging), but appearance is real not illusory
- [[Democritus of Abdera]] — admitted the void to save change, while honouring Parmenidean atoms
- [[Zeno of Elea]] — student, developed paradoxes in support of Parmenides
- [[Aristotle]] — developed potentiality/actuality to make change philosophically respectable
- [[Immanuel Kant]] — the question of how stable knowledge is possible in a changing world
