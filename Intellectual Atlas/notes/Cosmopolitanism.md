# Cosmopolitanism
`idea` | First articulated: c. 400 BCE | Contested: yes
**Traditions:** Stoicism · Political Philosophy · Ethics
**Primary thinker:** [[Zeno of Citium]]

---

## Summary
All human beings are members of a single community by virtue of their shared rationality. Coined by Diogenes the Cynic, developed by the Stoics into a full philosophy: all humans share in the divine Logos and are therefore equal in moral standing regardless of nationality or birth. Fed directly into natural law theory, human rights, and international law.

---

## Connections
- Philosophical basis: [[Logos]] — shared rationality as the ground of equal moral worth
- Developed by: [[Zeno of Citium]], [[Marcus Aurelius]]
- Forward connection: [[Immanuel Kant]] — "kingdom of ends", universal moral community
- Forward connection: Modern human rights tradition, international law (Grotius)
