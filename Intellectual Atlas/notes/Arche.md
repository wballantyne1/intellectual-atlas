# Arche
`idea` | First articulated: c. 585 BCE | Contested: yes
**Traditions:** Pre-Socratic Philosophy · Metaphysics · Cosmology
**Primary thinker:** [[Thales of Miletus]]

---

## Summary
The concept of the single fundamental principle or substance from which all things originate. Each Pre-Socratic philosopher proposed a different candidate. The concept itself — that there *is* a single originating principle — is the founding assumption of all Western physics and metaphysics.

---

## Proposed answers
- [[Thales of Miletus]]: water
- [[Anaximander of Miletus]]: the Apeiron (the boundless)
- [[Anaximenes of Miletus]]: air
- [[Heraclitus of Ephesus]]: fire (as process/flux)
- [[Pythagoras of Samos]]: number
- [[Democritus of Abdera]]: atoms
