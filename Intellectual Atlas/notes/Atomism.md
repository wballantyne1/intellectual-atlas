# Atomism
`idea` | First articulated: c. 430 BCE | Contested: no
**Traditions:** Pre-Socratic Philosophy · Materialism · Natural Philosophy · Physics
**Primary thinker:** [[Democritus of Abdera]]

---

## Summary
All reality consists of indivisible particles moving through void. All change is their mechanical rearrangement. All qualities are secondary — arising from atomic interactions with our senses, not properties of atoms themselves. Ignored for two millennia, vindicated by modern physics. One of the most prescient theories in intellectual history.

---

## Connections
- Proposed by: [[Democritus of Abdera]]
- Responds to: [[Parmenides of Elea]] — atoms are unchanging (Parmenidean), but void allows motion

- Vindicated by: Dalton's atomic theory (1808), 20th-century particle physics
