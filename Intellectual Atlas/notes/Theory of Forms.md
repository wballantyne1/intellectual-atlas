# Theory of Forms
`idea` | First articulated: c. 385 BCE | Contested: yes
**Traditions:** Platonism · Metaphysics · Epistemology
**Primary thinker:** [[Plato]]

---

## Summary
True reality consists of eternal, perfect, abstract entities — Forms — of which physical things are imperfect copies. The Form of Beauty is perfectly beautiful; particular beautiful things merely participate in it. Resolved the Heraclitus/Parmenides contradiction: Forms are Parmenidean (eternal, unchanging), the sensible world is Heraclitean (in flux).

## Connections
- Proposed by: [[Plato]]
- Synthesises: [[Parmenides of Elea]] + [[Heraclitus of Ephesus]]
- Draws on: [[Pythagoras of Samos]] — Forms are mathematical in character
- Contradicted by: [[Aristotle]] — the Third Man Argument; Forms must be immanent
