# Stoic Ethics
`idea` | First articulated: c. 300 BCE | Contested: no
**Traditions:** Stoicism · Ethics · Hellenistic Philosophy
**Primary thinker:** [[Zeno of Citium]]

---

## Summary
Virtue is the only genuine good. Everything else — wealth, health, reputation, life itself — is 'indifferent'. The quality of a life is determined entirely by the quality of the mind that lives it. We cannot control external events, only our responses. This radical internalism made Stoicism available to slave and emperor alike.
