# Plato
`thinker` | c. 428–348 BCE | Greek (Athenian)
**Traditions:** Classical Greek Philosophy · Platonism · Idealism

---

## Summary
The most influential philosopher in the Western tradition. Student of Socrates, teacher of Aristotle, founder of the Academy. His Theory of Forms synthesised the entire Pre-Socratic debate: Forms are eternal and unchanging (Parmenides), the sensible world is in flux (Heraclitus), and reality is fundamentally mathematical (Pythagoras). Whitehead: all Western philosophy is "footnotes to Plato."

---

## Influenced by
- [[Socrates]] — the dialogues dramatise Socratic conversation; his execution shaped Plato's entire project
- [[Parmenides of Elea]] — the Forms are eternal and unchanging
- [[Heraclitus of Ephesus]] — the sensible world is in constant flux
- [[Pythagoras of Samos]] — reality is fundamentally mathematical
- [[Egyptian Wisdom Tradition]] — reportedly studied in Egypt

## Ideas
- [[Theory of Forms]] — true reality is a realm of eternal, perfect abstractions
- [[Virtue Ethics]] — knowledge of the Good is required for genuine virtue

## Influenced
- [[Aristotle]] — twenty years at the Academy; systematic disagreement as much as inheritance
- [[Immanuel Kant]] — the Forms echo in the categories of the understanding
- [[Georg Wilhelm Friedrich Hegel]] — dialectical idealism as a transformation of Platonism

## Contradicted by
- [[Aristotle]] — Forms must be immanent in things, not transcendent above them
