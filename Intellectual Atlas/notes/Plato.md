# Plato
`thinker` | c. 428–348 BCE | Greek (Athenian)
**Traditions:** Classical Greek Philosophy · Platonism · Idealism

> **Stub** — full node not yet built

## Summary
Student of Socrates, teacher of Aristotle. Author of the dialogues — the most influential body of philosophical writing in Western history. His theory of Forms attempted to synthesise the Pre-Socratic debate: the Forms are real, eternal, and unchanging (Parmenidean); the sensible world participates in them imperfectly (saving appearances from mere illusion). His mathematical idealism owes an immense debt to Pythagoras.
