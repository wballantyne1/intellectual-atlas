## Summary
The dominant figure of 19th-century German philosophy. Hegel's entire system is a sustained response to Kant — accepting Kant's insight that subject and object are actively related, but dissolving Kant's boundary between appearances and things-in-themselves. History itself, for Hegel, is the process by which Geist (Spirit/Mind) comes to know itself.
