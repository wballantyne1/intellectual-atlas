# Ataraxia
`idea` | First articulated: c. 300 BCE | Contested: no
**Traditions:** Epicureanism · Hellenistic Philosophy · Ethics
**Primary thinker:** [[Epicurus]]

---

## Summary
The Epicurean ideal of tranquillity — an undisturbed, anxiety-free state achieved through eliminating unnecessary fears and cultivating simple pleasures. Not excitement or intensity, but steadiness. The cure is philosophical understanding: atomism dissolves fear of death; natural theology dissolves fear of divine punishment.

---

## Connections
- Proposed by: [[Epicurus]]
- Philosophical basis: [[Atomism]] — death is nothing to us; gods are indifferent
- Preserved by: [[Lucretius]]
- Contrasts with: [[Stoic Ethics]] — engagement vs. withdrawal as the ethical ideal
