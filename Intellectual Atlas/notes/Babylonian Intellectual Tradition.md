# Babylonian Intellectual Tradition
`tradition` | c. 3000 BCE – c. 500 BCE | Mesopotamia (modern Iraq)

---

## Summary
The intellectual tradition of ancient Mesopotamia produced the earliest known mathematical astronomy, systematic record-keeping, legal philosophy, and cosmogony. Their methods directly influenced early Greek natural philosophy — particularly through cultural contact at Miletus. Thales almost certainly used Babylonian eclipse-prediction data.

---

## Key Contributions
- Mathematical astronomy and eclipse prediction (saros cycles)
- Base-60 arithmetic — origin of degrees, minutes, seconds
- The Enuma Elish: one of the earliest cosmogony narratives
- Code of Hammurabi: systematic codified law
- Proto-algebra for practical problem-solving
