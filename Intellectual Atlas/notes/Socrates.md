# Socrates
`thinker` | c. 470–399 BCE | Greek (Athenian)
**Traditions:** Classical Greek Philosophy · Ethics · Dialectic

---

## Summary
The founder of Western ethical philosophy — and one of history's most paradoxical figures. He wrote nothing, claimed to know nothing, and was executed for his ideas. His method — relentless questioning to expose hidden assumptions — permanently changed what philosophy is. Everything we know of him is filtered through others, primarily Plato.

---

## Context
- Turned philosophy from nature (Pre-Socratics) toward ethics and self-knowledge
- Executed 399 BCE: charged with impiety and corrupting Athenian youth
- His death was the event that shaped Plato's entire life's work

## Ideas
- [[Virtue Ethics]] — virtue is knowledge; wrongdoing is always a form of ignorance
- The Socratic method — elenchus as a tool for exposing false certainty

## Influenced
- [[Plato]] — the dialogues, the Theory of Forms, the entire Platonic project
