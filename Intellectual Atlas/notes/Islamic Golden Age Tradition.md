# Islamic Golden Age Tradition
`tradition` | c. 750–1258 CE | Middle East, North Africa, Iberia

> **Stub** — full node not yet built

## Summary
The period of extraordinary intellectual flourishing in the Islamic world — translating, preserving, and extending Greek philosophy, mathematics, astronomy, and medicine. Without the Islamic Golden Age, Aristotle's works would likely have been lost to Western Europe. Figures including Averroes, Avicenna, and Al-Ghazali transformed the Greek inheritance and returned it to Europe transformed.
