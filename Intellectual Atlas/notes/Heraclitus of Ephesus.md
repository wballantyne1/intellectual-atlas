# Heraclitus of Ephesus
`thinker` | c. 535–475 BCE | Greek (Ionian)
**Traditions:** Pre-Socratic Philosophy · Natural Philosophy

---

## Summary
The philosopher of flux and fire. Everything changes continuously; apparent stability is an illusion. Beneath the flux is the Logos — a rational principle governing all change. Stands in direct contradiction to Parmenides, who argued that change is itself the illusion. Their confrontation is the first great philosophical disagreement in Western thought.
