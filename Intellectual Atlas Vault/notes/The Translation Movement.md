# The Translation Movement

**c. 750–900 CE | Baghdad**

The systematic, state-sponsored project to translate Greek, Persian, and Indian texts into Arabic — centred on Baghdad's House of Wisdom. One of the most consequential intellectual projects in history.

The simplest way to grasp its importance: without it, we would likely have lost most of Aristotle. When the Greek originals were destroyed or lost in Byzantium, these Arabic translations — and their subsequent Latin versions — became the only surviving record.

## How it worked

Under the Abbasid Caliphs — particularly al-Mamun (813–833 CE) — the House of Wisdom became the world's leading translation bureau. Translators were often Syriac Christian scholars working alongside Arab philosophers, paid handsomely for their work. The story goes that al-Mamun paid translators the weight of their manuscript in gold.

[[Al-Kindi]] both directed and philosophically extended this effort — he wasn't merely translating but actively synthesising what he translated into original Arabic philosophy.

## Why it matters

- Aristotle's *Physics*, *Metaphysics*, *Ethics*, *Logic*, *De Anima* — all translated into Arabic
- Plato's dialogues, Ptolemy's astronomy, Euclid's geometry, Galen's medicine — all preserved here
- When Latin translations later flowed into European universities, they came via Arabic, not directly from Greek
- The intellectual chain runs: Athens → Baghdad → Córdoba → Paris
