# Thomas Aquinas

**1225–1274 CE | Italian | Roccasecca**

> Stub — full node not yet built

The defining philosopher-theologian of medieval Christian Europe. Aquinas synthesised Aristotelian philosophy with Christian theology, creating the intellectual framework that dominated Catholic thought for centuries.

His *Summa Theologica* was written partly as a direct response to [[Averroes]] — specifically to refute the Averroist claim that there is no personal immortality.
