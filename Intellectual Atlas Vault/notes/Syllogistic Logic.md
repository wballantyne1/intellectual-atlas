# Syllogistic Logic
`idea` | First articulated: c. 350 BCE | Contested: no
**Traditions:** Classical Greek Philosophy · Logic · Philosophy of Language
**Primary thinker:** [[Aristotle]]

---

## Summary
Aristotle's invention of formal deductive logic. "All men are mortal; Socrates is a man; therefore Socrates is mortal." For the first time, the *form* of a valid argument was separated from its content and analysed in its own right. Dominant framework for logic for two thousand years — until Frege's predicate logic in 1879.

---

## Connections
- Invented by: [[Aristotle]]
- Transmitted via: [[Islamic Golden Age Tradition]] (stub) — Arabic translations kept it alive in Europe's early medieval period
- Superseded by: Frege's Begriffsschrift (1879) — predicate logic handles relations and mathematics syllogistic cannot
- Backdrop to: [[Immanuel Kant]] — the Critique of Pure Reason assumes syllogistic as the model of formal reasoning
