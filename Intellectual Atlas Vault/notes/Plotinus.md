# Plotinus
`thinker` | c. 204–270 CE | Egyptian-Roman
**Traditions:** Neoplatonism · Mysticism · Late Antique Philosophy

---

## Summary
Founder of Neoplatonism. Born in Egypt, educated in Alexandria, taught in Rome. Fused Plato's philosophy with mystical and Oriental elements into a grand vision: all reality emanates from a single source — the One — through successive levels down to matter. Became the philosophical backbone of both Christian theology and Islamic philosophy.

---

## Influenced by
- [[Plato]] — radicalised the Form of the Good into the One
- [[Egyptian Wisdom Tradition]] — mystical framework, Alexandrian synthesis

## Ideas
- [[The One]] — the absolute, simple source of all reality
- [[Emanation]] — how the Many proceeds necessarily from the One
- [[Theory of Forms]] — inherited and restructured as Nous

## Influenced
- [[Al-Kindi]] — Plotinus's concept of the One became Al-Kindi's First Cause, identified with Allah
- [[Avicenna]] — Neoplatonist emanation framework shaped Avicenna's ten cosmic intelligences
- [[Islamic Golden Age]] — via the Theology of Aristotle (a Plotinus text misattributed to Aristotle in Arabic translation)
